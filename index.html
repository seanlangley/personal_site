Book Review

Brief History of Time -- Stephen Hawking

Great book. It is so fascinating to read about a man so legendary that his name really transcends our world. Almost like Albert Einstein. At this point in time, it means something completely different to call someone an "Einstein" than it did one hundred years ago.
This was a great book. It takes a really intelligent man to be able to explain concepts of astrophysics and string theory in ways that ordinary people can understand. Hawkning even talks about the value of concisely explaining your thoughts. He says that the most valuable scientific theories are those that can be universally understood. He says that the value of a scientific theory diminishes when it takes an expert many years of experience to understand a subject.
Another point that Hawking made that had a major impact on me was his discussion of scientific theories. Hawking makes the argument that scientific theories are nothing more than models which seem to match our observations. The better the scientific theory, the more accurately we can predict future events based on those theories. He then explains how, with our current understanding of physics, many of our models are incorrect (to a certain degree). Newton's model of gravity, for example, was thought to be truth for several hundred years. And it matched our observations of the movement of the planets to a very close degree. The only problem with this model was that it failed to accurately predict the movement of Mercury. Scientists failed to understand why until Albert Einstein completely reconstructed the model of gravity into the theory of special relativity, something even more universal.
The major takeaway that I have from this book is to always be skeptical. Theories are only as true as the observations which they help to predict.
